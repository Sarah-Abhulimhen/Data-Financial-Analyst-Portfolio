# Financial Analysis – The Coca-Cola Company

## Executive Summary

This project evaluates the financial performance of The Coca-Cola Company over the period 2020–2023 to assess its suitability as an investment opportunity.

Using the CORE framework (Context, Overview, Ratios, Evaluation), the analysis combines financial statement review with ratio analysis across profitability, efficiency, liquidity, and capital structure.

The findings show that Coca-Cola delivered strong post-pandemic recovery, with revenue increasing from $33.0 billion in 2020 to $45.8 billion in 2023 (over 39% growth). Profitability improved in terms of capital efficiency, as ROCE rose from 12.38% to 15.26%, although operating margins declined slightly due to rising input costs.

Liquidity remained stable, while gearing reduced from 71% to 63%, indicating improved financial stability and lower reliance on debt. Efficiency also improved, with receivable days decreasing from 35 to 27 days, demonstrating stronger cash collection.

Overall, the analysis concludes that Coca-Cola is a financially strong and stable company with consistent growth, efficient capital utilisation, and solid investment potential despite moderate cost pressures.

## Business Problem
Should Software Solutions invest in The Coca-Cola Company based on its financial performance, efficiency, and long-term growth potential over the past four years (2020–2023)?

## Methodology
- Analysed financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Used Microsoft Excel to organise and evaluate data
- Calculated key financial ratios including:
  - Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)
  - Operating profit margin
  - Current ratio
  - Gearing ratio
- Considered external factors such as inflation and post-pandemic recovery

## Key Insights

* Revenue grew by approximately 39% from $33.0bn (2020) to $45.8bn (2023), driven by post-pandemic recovery, global expansion, and pricing strategies
* ROCE increased from 12.38% to 15.26%, indicating improved efficiency in generating returns from invested capital
* Operating profit rose from $8.99bn to $11.31bn, but operating margins declined from 27.3% to 24.7%, reflecting rising production and input costs
* Net profit showed volatility, dropping by 20.77% in 2021 before recovering to $10.7bn in 2023, highlighting sensitivity to external shocks
* Liquidity remained stable, with the current ratio holding around 1.13–1.32, indicating sufficient short-term financial strength
* Gearing reduced from 71% to 63%, demonstrating a strategic reduction in long-term debt and improved financial risk management
* Efficiency improved significantly, with receivable days falling from 35 to 27 days, strengthening cash flow management

## Business Recommendation
Based on the analysis, Coca-Cola demonstrates strong financial performance and stability, making it a suitable investment option. 

## Other Business Recommendations

Based on the financial analysis, the following strategic recommendations are proposed to enhance Coca-Cola’s long-term performance:

* **Strengthen Cost Management to Improve Margins**
  Although revenue growth was strong, declining operating margins indicate rising cost pressures. Coca-Cola should implement stricter cost-control measures, particularly in production, logistics, and raw material sourcing, to protect profitability.

* **Leverage Strong ROCE for Strategic Investments**
  Improved Return on Capital Employed suggests efficient use of resources. The company should reinvest in high-return areas such as product innovation, digital transformation, and emerging markets to sustain growth momentum.

* **Enhance Operational Efficiency Through Technology**
  Investing in automation, supply chain optimisation, and data analytics can reduce operational inefficiencies and improve overall performance.

* **Maintain Liquidity While Optimising Capital Structure**
  Stable liquidity positions the company well for short-term obligations. However, Coca-Cola should ensure an optimal balance between debt and equity to minimise financial risk while supporting expansion.

* **Expand Product Diversification and Market Reach**
  To sustain long-term growth, Coca-Cola should continue diversifying its product portfolio (e.g. healthier beverages) and expanding in high-growth international markets.
